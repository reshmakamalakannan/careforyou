Menstrual Hygiene Is Key in Promoting Good Health



Wear lightweight, breathable clothing

From picking the right underwear to avoiding leakage, there are a lot of factors to consider when it comes to finding the perfect period outfit.Wear lightweight, breathable clothing (such as cotton underwear). Tight fabrics can trap moisture and heat, allowing germs to thrive. Cotton pieces are durable, comfy, and airy. These pieces let your skin breathe, and never cling. Whatever you decide to do, no matter the weather

Change your menstrual products regularly.

Trapped moisture provides a breeding ground for bacteria and fungi. Wearing a pad or period underwear for too long can lead to a rash or an infection. Cotton is a highly breathable fiber that absorbs a decent amount of moisture and has a fairly open weave which allows air to circulate through the fabric. It’s safe to say that cotton is the world’s favorite natural fiber.

Keep your genital area clean

Wash the outside of your vagina and bottom every day. When you go to the bathroom, wipe from the front of your body toward the back, not the other way. Use only water to rinse your vulva. The vagina is a self-cleaning organ. Changing the natural pH balance of your vagina by washing or using chemicals to cleanse out the vagina can be harmful and may result in yeast infection or bacterial vaginosis.

Use unscented tampons and pads

Unscented is best because the chemicals used in scented products can cause irritation. Also, there's no need to mask the natural odors that happen. In most cases, any odor is mild and not offensive. If you do notice a smell that seems wrong or bad to you, you might want to talk to your doctor about it. Some odors are signs of infections.

Visit a healthcare provider for your annual check-up.

Visit a healthcare provider for your annual check-up. An annual well-woman exam is a full check-up includes a pap smear, a pelvic exam, and a breast exam. These exams are essential for good reproductive health as they can catch early signs of cancer or other health issues.
